About


Reading could help you live longer. People who read books live for around two years longer than those who don't, irrespective of gender, wealth, education or health, according to a Yale University study. ... “Compared to non-book readers, book readers had a 23-month survival advantage,” they noted. Reading increases our linguistic skills. The more we read, the more vocabulary we're exposed to. This helps us become more articulate whether we're speaking face to face with others or writing our thoughts down on a page. The more facts, situations, vocabulary, and perspectives we come across, the smarter we become. Learning to read is about listening and understanding as well as working out what is printed on the page. Through hearing stories, children are exposed to a wide range of words. This helps them build their own vocabulary and improve their understanding when they listen, which is vital as they start to read.
